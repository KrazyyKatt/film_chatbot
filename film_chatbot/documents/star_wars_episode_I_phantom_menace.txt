OVERVIEW
--------
Star Wars: Episode I – The Phantom Menace is the first chapter in the
chronological order of the Star Wars saga, set 32 years before the events of
the original trilogy. It is the first installment of the prequel trilogy and
the fourth Star Wars film to be released overall. The film marks George Lucas's
return to directing after a 22-year hiatus following the original Star Wars
(1977).

The story takes place during the era of the Galactic Republic and follows
Jedi Master Qui-Gon Jinn and his apprentice Obi-Wan Kenobi as they try to
protect Queen Padmé Amidala of Naboo during a dangerous political and military
crisis. Along the way, they discover a young boy named Anakin Skywalker, who
is strong with the Force — the same boy who will eventually become Darth Vader.
The film also introduces the sinister return of the Sith, an ancient order of
dark-side Force users believed to have been extinct for over a thousand years.


PLOT SUMMARY
------------
The Trade Federation, secretly manipulated by the mysterious Sith Lord Darth
Sidious, has blockaded the peaceful planet of Naboo in preparation for a full
military invasion. The Supreme Chancellor of the Galactic Republic, Finis
Valorum, sends two Jedi — Master Qui-Gon Jinn and his young apprentice
Obi-Wan Kenobi — to negotiate a peaceful resolution with the Trade Federation's
Viceroy, Nute Gunray.

Upon arriving, the two Jedi are nearly killed on Darth Sidious's orders. They
escape to the surface of Naboo, where the invasion has already begun. During
their escape through the planet's swamps, Qui-Gon rescues Jar Jar Binks, a
clumsy outcast from the amphibious Gungan species. With Jar Jar's help, they
travel underwater to Theed, Naboo's capital city, and rescue the planet's young
queen, Padmé Amidala. The group flees Naboo aboard the queen's royal starship,
hoping to reach Coruscant — the Republic's capital planet — to seek help.

During their escape, the ship is damaged passing through the Trade Federation
blockade. It is saved by a small astromech droid named R2-D2. Unable to make
the full journey to Coruscant, the ship is forced to land on the remote desert
planet of Tatooine, far outside the Republic's reach, for emergency repairs.

On Tatooine, Qui-Gon, Jar Jar, R2-D2, and Padmé (disguised as a simple
handmaiden for her own protection) visit the settlement of Mos Espa to find
the parts they need. There, they meet Watto, a junk dealer, and his young
slave: a nine-year-old boy named Anakin Skywalker. Anakin is remarkably gifted
— a skilled pilot and engineer who has even built his own protocol droid, C-3PO.
Qui-Gon senses an extraordinary connection to the Force within the boy and
comes to believe Anakin may be the "Chosen One" — a prophesied figure destined
to bring balance to the Force.

Since Watto refuses to accept Republic currency as payment, Qui-Gon bets on
Anakin competing in a dangerous high-speed podrace — wagering the needed
hyperdrive part and Anakin's freedom. Against all odds, Anakin wins the race.
He is freed from slavery and joins the group, leaving behind his mother, Shmi
Skywalker, in one of the film's most emotional scenes.

As they make their way back to their ship, the group is attacked by Darth Maul
— a fearsome Sith warrior and apprentice to Darth Sidious — who was sent to
recapture Queen Amidala. Qui-Gon duels Maul briefly before the group escapes.

Upon arriving on Coruscant, Padmé addresses the Galactic Senate to plead for
help for Naboo, but finds the Senate too mired in bureaucracy and corruption
to act quickly. Meanwhile, Senator Palpatine — Naboo's own representative —
subtly manipulates Padmé into calling for a vote of no confidence against
Chancellor Valorum, which triggers a new election. Palpatine himself is
nominated as a candidate for Supreme Chancellor.

Qui-Gon presents Anakin to the Jedi Council and requests permission to train
him as a Jedi. The Council, including Grand Master Yoda and Mace Windu, refuses.
They sense great potential in Anakin, but also great fear — and warn that his
fear could make him vulnerable to the dark side of the Force. Undeterred, Qui-
Gon vows to train Anakin himself regardless.

Frustrated with the Senate's inaction, Padmé decides to return to Naboo and
fight to free her people herself. The Jedi are instructed by the Council to
accompany her and investigate the Sith threat. Back on Naboo, Padmé reveals her
true identity as the queen to the Gungans, and forges an alliance with their
leader, Boss Nass. Together, they plan a three-pronged assault on the Trade
Federation.

The battle unfolds on three fronts simultaneously:
  1. The Gungan army engages the Trade Federation's droid army on the open
     plains as a distraction.
  2. Padmé leads a strike team into Theed Palace to capture Viceroy Nute Gunray.
  3. Anakin accidentally activates the autopilot on a Naboo starfighter and
     ends up in space, joining the fight against the Trade Federation's Droid
     Control Ship. In a lucky but pivotal moment, he accidentally destroys the
     ship's reactor from the inside, which deactivates the entire droid army.

Meanwhile, inside Theed Palace, Darth Maul confronts both Qui-Gon and Obi-Wan
in an intense three-way lightsaber duel. During the battle, Maul mortally wounds
Qui-Gon. An enraged Obi-Wan then defeats Maul, cutting him in half. He rushes
to Qui-Gon's side, but it is too late. With his last breath, Qui-Gon asks
Obi-Wan to promise to train Anakin as a Jedi.

In the aftermath, Nute Gunray is arrested. Palpatine is elected the new Supreme
Chancellor. The Jedi Council promotes Obi-Wan to the rank of Jedi Knight, and —
despite earlier reservations — grants him permission to train Anakin as his
apprentice. At Qui-Gon's funeral, Yoda quietly voices concern: given the ancient
Sith rule of two (a master and an apprentice), if Maul was the apprentice, who
was the master?

The film ends with a celebration on Naboo, honoring the alliance between the
Naboo people and the Gungans, and Padmé presenting Boss Nass with a symbol of
peace.


MAIN CAST
---------
Liam Neeson       — Qui-Gon Jinn
                    A wise and independent-minded Jedi Master who discovers
                    Anakin Skywalker and believes him to be the Chosen One.
                    Despite the Jedi Council's refusal, he vows to train Anakin.

Ewan McGregor     — Obi-Wan Kenobi
                    Qui-Gon's 25-year-old Padawan (apprentice). Loyal,
                    disciplined, and capable, he ultimately fulfills his
                    master's dying wish by taking Anakin as his apprentice.

Natalie Portman   — Padmé Amidala
                    The 14-year-old Queen of Naboo. Brave, politically sharp,
                    and determined to free her people. She disguises herself as
                    a handmaiden for most of the film to conceal her identity.

Jake Lloyd        — Anakin Skywalker
                    A nine-year-old slave boy on Tatooine with extraordinary
                    Force abilities. Joyful and gifted, he has no idea of the
                    dark destiny that awaits him.

Ian McDiarmid     — Senator Palpatine / Darth Sidious
                    The seemingly gentle and helpful senator from Naboo, who
                    is secretly Darth Sidious — the Sith master behind the
                    entire invasion. He is the true "Phantom Menace" of the title.

Ahmed Best        — Jar Jar Binks
                    A clumsy, exiled Gungan who joins the heroes and serves
                    as comic relief throughout the film.

Pernilla August   — Shmi Skywalker
                    Anakin's devoted mother, who selflessly allows her son
                    to leave with the Jedi in hopes of a better life.

Ray Park          — Darth Maul
                    Darth Sidious's fearsome Sith apprentice, recognizable
                    by his red and black tattoos and double-bladed lightsaber.

Frank Oz          — Yoda (voice & puppet)
                    The centuries-old Grand Master of the Jedi Order. Wise and
                    cautious, he senses both great power and great danger in Anakin.

Anthony Daniels   — C-3PO
                    A protocol droid built by Anakin himself, appearing without
                    his iconic golden plating in this film.

Kenny Baker       — R2-D2
                    An astromech droid from Naboo whose quick thinking saves
                    the queen's ship during the escape from Naboo.

Samuel L. Jackson — Mace Windu
                    A senior member of the Jedi Council, skeptical of training
                    Anakin.


THEMES
------
- The corrupting influence of fear and how it can lead to the dark side
- Political manipulation and the slow erosion of democracy
- Destiny and prophecy (the "Chosen One" arc)
- The parent-child bond (Anakin and Shmi, Qui-Gon and Obi-Wan)
- Slavery and freedom


PRODUCTION NOTES
----------------
George Lucas began writing The Phantom Menace in 1993, inspired by the
backstories he had developed for the characters of the original trilogy —
particularly Anakin Skywalker's fall from grace. Production began in 1994, and
filming ran from June to September 1997 at:
  - Leavesden Film Studios (UK)
  - The Royal Palace of Caserta, Italy (used as Coruscant interiors)
  - The Tunisian desert (Tatooine exterior scenes)

The film was a landmark in the use of computer-generated imagery (CGI). Almost
all environments and many characters — including Jar Jar Binks, who became the
first fully CGI main character in a major film — were digitally created.

The score was composed by John Williams, featuring the iconic piece
"Duel of the Fates," which plays during the climactic lightsaber battle with
Darth Maul and has become one of the most recognizable pieces in film music.


RECEPTION
---------
Critical reception was mixed upon release. The film was widely praised for:
  - Its groundbreaking visual effects and CGI
  - The action sequences, especially the podrace and the final lightsaber duel
  - John Williams's musical score

It was criticized for:
  - Its heavy reliance on exposition and political plot lines
  - The characterization of Jar Jar Binks, who became a symbol of controversy
  - The portrayal of Anakin Skywalker as overly innocent and child-like

Despite mixed reviews, the film was a massive box-office success. During its
initial theatrical run it grossed $924 million worldwide, making it:
  - The highest-grossing film of 1999
  - The second-highest-grossing film of all time at that point (behind Titanic)
  - The highest-grossing Star Wars film at the time

A 3D re-release in 2012 pushed its lifetime gross over $1 billion.


PLACE IN THE SAGA
-----------------
The Phantom Menace is Episode I of the Skywalker Saga. It is chronologically
the first story but was the fourth film produced. It was followed by:
  - Star Wars: Episode II – Attack of the Clones (2002)
  - Star Wars: Episode III – Revenge of the Sith (2005)

These three films form the prequel trilogy, which chronicles the rise and fall
of Anakin Skywalker and the destruction of the Jedi Order, setting the stage
for the original trilogy (Episodes IV, V, and VI).
