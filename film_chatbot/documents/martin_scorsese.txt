PERSONAL INFORMATION
---------------------
Full name:      Martin Charles Scorsese
Born:           November 17, 1942 – Flushing, Queens, New York City, U.S.
Age:            83
Nationality:    American / Italian
Education:      New York University (BA in English, 1964; MA, 1968)
Occupations:    Director, Producer, Screenwriter, Actor, Film Historian
Years active:   1962 – present
Children:       3 (including Domenica and Francesca)


OVERVIEW
--------
Martin Scorsese is widely regarded as one of the greatest and most influential
film directors in the history of cinema. A central figure of the New Hollywood
movement of the 1970s, his work is deeply shaped by his Italian-American
background and his upbringing in the Little Italy neighborhood of Manhattan.
His films frequently explore themes of crime, guilt, redemption, masculinity,
Catholic faith, and the dark side of the American Dream.

His career spans more than fifty years, during which he has directed 26 feature-
length narrative films, 17 feature-length documentary films, and co-directed
one anthology film. He is especially known for his long-running creative
partnerships with actors Robert De Niro and Leonardo DiCaprio.

He has received numerous major accolades including:
  - Academy Award (Best Director, The Departed, 2007)
  - 4 BAFTA Awards
  - 3 Emmy Awards
  - 1 Grammy Award
  - 3 Golden Globe Awards
  - AFI Life Achievement Award (1997)
  - Kennedy Center Honor (2007)
  - Cecil B. DeMille Award (2010)
  - BAFTA Fellowship (2012)
  - Palme d'Or at Cannes (Taxi Driver, 1976)


EARLY LIFE
----------
Scorsese was born in Queens and raised in the Little Italy neighborhood of
Manhattan. Both his parents, Charles and Catherine Scorsese, worked in the
Garment District, and all four of his grandparents were Sicilian immigrants.
The family surname was originally "Scozzese" (meaning "Scot" in Italian), but
was changed due to a transcription error.

As a child, Scorsese suffered from severe asthma and was unable to play sports
or participate in outdoor activities with other children. As a result, his
family frequently took him to the cinema, where he developed a deep and lasting
passion for film. He was especially affected by the works of Powell and
Pressburger, Jean Renoir, Alfred Hitchcock, Ingmar Bergman, Federico Fellini,
and the French New Wave movement.

Scorsese initially considered becoming a Catholic priest and attended a
preparatory seminary, but left after one year. He enrolled at New York
University, earning a bachelor's degree in English (1964) and a Master of Arts
(1968). During his studies at NYU's Tisch School of the Arts, he made several
short films, including the darkly comic "The Big Shave" (1967), an anti-Vietnam
War allegory. He has credited his film professor Haig P. Manoogian as one of
his most formative influences, calling his teaching "the most precious gift I
have ever received."


DIRECTORIAL STYLE & THEMES
---------------------------
Scorsese's films are recognizable by several recurring stylistic traits:
  - Extensive use of slow motion and freeze frames
  - Voice-over narration
  - Graphic depictions of violence
  - Liberal use of profanity
  - Morally complex characters caught between sin and redemption
  - Deep use of popular music to underscore emotional moments

Recurring themes across his work include:
  - Crime, loyalty, and betrayal
  - Catholic guilt and the search for redemption
  - Masculinity and violence in American culture
  - The price of ambition and greed
  - Faith, doubt, and spiritual struggle

FILMOGRAPHY – NARRATIVE FEATURE FILMS
================================================================================

1967  Who's That Knocking at My Door
      Director, Writer
      Independent drama. Scorsese's debut feature, exploring the conflict
      between Catholic guilt and sexual desire in a young Italian-American man
      from New York's Little Italy.

1972  Boxcar Bertha
      Director
      Romance drama. A Depression-era outlaw story produced by Roger Corman.

1973  Mean Streets
      Director, Writer (co-written with Mardik Martin)
      Crime drama. Set in Little Italy, New York. First collaboration with
      Robert De Niro. Follows a small-time hood trying to protect his reckless
      friend while being pulled between street life and his Catholic conscience.
      Often cited among the greatest films ever made.

1974  Alice Doesn't Live Here Anymore
      Director
      Romantic comedy-drama. A widow travels across the American Southwest
      with her young son, trying to rebuild her life as a singer.

1976  Taxi Driver
      Director
      Psychological drama / thriller. Robert De Niro stars as Travis Bickle, a
      mentally unstable Vietnam veteran working as a New York City cab driver
      who becomes obsessed with rescuing a young prostitute. Won the Palme d'Or
      at the 1976 Cannes Film Festival. One of the most celebrated films in
      American cinema history.

1977  New York, New York
      Director
      Musical drama. A nostalgic homage to classic Hollywood musicals, starring
      Robert De Niro and Liza Minnelli as musicians in a turbulent romance.

1980  Raging Bull
      Director
      Biographical sports drama. Robert De Niro plays real-life middleweight
      boxing champion Jake LaMotta, whose self-destructive rage destroys his
      career and relationships. Widely considered one of the greatest films ever
      made. De Niro won the Academy Award for Best Actor.

1982  The King of Comedy
      Director
      Satirical black comedy. Robert De Niro plays an obsessive, delusional
      aspiring stand-up comedian who kidnaps a famous talk show host (Jerry
      Lewis). A dark commentary on fame and celebrity culture, seen by many as
      ahead of its time.

1985  After Hours
      Director
      Black comedy / thriller. A dark, surreal nightmare comedy following a
      man's increasingly bizarre and dangerous night in downtown Manhattan.

1986  The Color of Money
      Director
      Sports drama. Sequel to The Hustler (1961). Paul Newman reprises his role
      as pool shark "Fast Eddie" Felson, now mentoring a young player (Tom
      Cruise). Newman won the Academy Award for Best Actor.

1988  The Last Temptation of Christ
      Director
      Religious epic. A controversial adaptation of Nikos Kazantzakis's novel,
      imagining Jesus Christ experiencing human doubt, fear, and temptation.
      Co-written with Jay Cocks.

1990  Goodfellas
      Director, Writer (co-written with Nicholas Pileggi)
      Crime drama. Based on the true story of Henry Hill, a mobster who rises
      through the ranks of the Lucchese crime family over three decades before
      becoming an FBI informant. Starring Ray Liotta, Robert De Niro, and
      Joe Pesci. Widely regarded as one of the greatest films ever made.

1991  Cape Fear
      Director
      Psychological thriller. A remake of the 1962 film. Robert De Niro plays
      a menacing ex-convict terrorizing the family of the lawyer (Nick Nolte)
      he believes wronged him.

1993  The Age of Innocence
      Director, Writer (co-written with Jay Cocks)
      Period romance. Based on Edith Wharton's Pulitzer Prize-winning novel.
      Set in 1870s New York high society, exploring a passionate but impossible
      love affair between a lawyer and a scandalous woman.

1995  Casino
      Director, Writer (co-written with Nicholas Pileggi)
      Crime drama. Based on the true story of the mob's control over Las Vegas
      casinos in the 1970s and 1980s. Starring Robert De Niro, Sharon Stone,
      and Joe Pesci.

1997  Kundun
      Director
      Biographical drama. Chronicles the early life of the 14th Dalai Lama,
      from his childhood discovery as a reincarnation to his exile from Tibet
      after the Chinese invasion.

1999  Bringing Out the Dead
      Director
      Drama / dark comedy. Based on the novel by Joe Connelly. Nicolas Cage
      plays an exhausted New York paramedic haunted by the ghosts of people he
      failed to save.

2002  Gangs of New York
      Director
      Historical drama. Set in 1863 New York City, in the violent Five Points
      slum neighborhood. Leonardo DiCaprio stars as an Irish immigrant seeking
      revenge against the ruthless gang leader (Daniel Day-Lewis) who killed
      his father. First collaboration with DiCaprio.

2004  The Aviator
      Director
      Biographical drama. Leonardo DiCaprio plays Howard Hughes, the eccentric
      billionaire aviator and filmmaker, tracing his rise to fame and his
      descent into severe obsessive-compulsive disorder.

2006  The Departed
      Director
      Crime thriller. An undercover cop infiltrates the Irish mob in Boston,
      while the mob simultaneously plants a mole in the police department.
      Starring Leonardo DiCaprio, Matt Damon, Jack Nicholson, and Mark Wahlberg.
      Won the Academy Award for Best Picture and Best Director.

2010  Shutter Island
      Director, Producer
      Neo-noir psychological thriller. Leonardo DiCaprio plays a U.S. Marshal
      investigating a disappearance at a remote psychiatric facility, where
      nothing is as it seems.

2011  Hugo
      Director, Producer
      Children's adventure / fantasy. Set in 1930s Paris, a young orphan living
      inside a train station gets caught up in a mystery involving an automaton
      and a forgotten film pioneer (Georges Méliès). Won 5 Academy Awards.

2013  The Wolf of Wall Street
      Director, Producer
      Black comedy crime film. Based on the memoir of Jordan Belfort. Leonardo
      DiCaprio plays a stockbroker who builds a fraudulent Wall Street empire
      fueled by greed, drugs, and corruption. One of the highest-grossing films
      of Scorsese's career.

2016  Silence
      Director, Writer, Producer (co-written with Jay Cocks)
      Religious epic. Based on Shūsaku Endō's novel. Two 17th-century Portuguese
      Jesuit priests (Andrew Garfield, Adam Driver) travel to Japan to find
      their missing mentor (Liam Neeson) during a time of brutal persecution
      of Christians. A deeply personal project for Scorsese, over 25 years in
      the making.

2019  The Irishman
      Director, Producer
      Gangster epic. Robert De Niro plays Frank Sheeran, a hitman and labor
      union official who claims to have been involved in the disappearance of
      Jimmy Hoffa (Al Pacino). Notable for the use of digital de-aging
      technology. Produced by Netflix.

2023  Killers of the Flower Moon
      Director, Writer, Producer (co-written with Eric Roth)
      Historical western / crime drama. Based on David Grann's non-fiction book.
      Set in 1920s Oklahoma, depicting the systematic murders of the Osage
      Nation members after oil was discovered on their land, and the early
      investigation by the FBI. Stars Leonardo DiCaprio and Robert De Niro.
      Running time: 206 minutes.

TBA   What Happens at Night
      Director, Producer
      Currently in production.

FILM PRESERVATION & LEGACY
================================================================================

Scorsese is a passionate advocate for film preservation. He has founded three
non-profit organizations dedicated to preserving cinema history:

  - The Film Foundation (1990) – dedicated to preserving and restoring
    endangered films from studios and archives worldwide.
  - The World Cinema Foundation (2007) – focused on preserving films from
    developing countries that might otherwise be lost.
  - The African Film Heritage Project (2017) – a collaboration with UNESCO
    and the Pan African Federation of Filmmakers to restore African cinema.

Five of his own films have been inducted into the National Film Registry by
the Library of Congress as "culturally, historically or aesthetically
significant."
